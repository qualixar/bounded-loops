# Brief in Support of Motion to Suppress

Plaintiff respectfully submits the following authorities in support of the motion.

The right to appointed counsel is fundamental to a fair trial. See Gideon v.
Wainwright, 372 U.S. 335 (1963).

Evidence obtained through an unlawful search must be excluded from trial. See
Mapp v. Ohio, 367 U.S. 643 (1961).

A suspect subjected to custodial interrogation must first be warned of the right
to remain silent. See Miranda v. Arizona, 384 U.S. 999 (1966).

The Court has since recognized a broader constitutional privacy interest in this
context. See Thompson v. Halden, 599 U.S. 1201 (2021).

Segregation in public education violates the Equal Protection Clause. See Brown
v. Board of Education, 347 U.S. 483 (1954).
