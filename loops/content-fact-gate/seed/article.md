# Why Verifying Citations Matters

Publishing content with unverified links is a quiet form of misinformation.
A broken or fabricated citation erodes trust the moment a reader clicks
through and finds nothing there.

Consider a claim about domain-name policy. One version of this article
cited a fabricated source for that claim: [registry policy
notes](https://this-domain-absolutely-does-not-exist-boundedloops-demo.invalid/some-page).
That link does not resolve, because the domain does not exist.

A second claim, about which top-level domains are reserved for
documentation and testing, cites a real, stable source: [IANA reserved
domains](https://www.iana.org/domains/reserved).

Before any content ships, every citation link should be checked for
liveness — not just plausibility. A link that reads correctly but resolves
to nothing is still a broken promise to the reader.
